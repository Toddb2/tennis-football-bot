How it works
The hedge bet locks in the same P&L whether the selection wins or loses. You always bet the opposite side to your entry (BACK entry → LAY to hedge; LAY entry → BACK to hedge).
Formula
hedgeStake   = (Stake × EntryOdds) / CurrentOdds
lockedProfit = Stake × (EntryOdds − CurrentOdds) / CurrentOdds  ← BACK
lockedProfit = Stake × (CurrentOdds − EntryOdds) / CurrentOdds  ← LAY
Example — BACK entry, odds shorten
You backed at 3.00 with £2 stake. Odds have shortened to 2.20.
hedgeStake   = (2 × 3.00) / 2.20 = £2.73 LAY
lockedProfit = 2 × (3.00 − 2.20) / 2.20 = +£0.73 guaranteed
If it wins: back pays £4 − lay costs £3.27 = +£0.73.  If it loses: lose £2 back stake + lay returns £2.73 = +£0.73.
Example — LAY entry, odds drift
You laid at 1.30 with £2 stake. Odds have drifted to 1.50.
hedgeStake   = (2 × 1.30) / 1.50 = £1.73 BACK
lockedProfit = 2 × (1.50 − 1.30) / 1.50 = +£0.27 guaranteed
When you lay and the odds drift, you can now back at a higher price to lock in profit — you win both ways.
Profit is positive when the market moves in your favour (BACK: odds shorten; LAY: odds drift). If odds move against you, the locked P&L will be negative — you're cutting a loss, not banking a profit.
